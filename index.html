Mohammad Raihan Uddin

Ph.D. Student in Computer Engineering

Research Interests: ML, AI, and Wireless Communications

I am currently a Ph.D. student in Computer Engineering at Clemson University in the Holcombe Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering. My doctoral research is carried out in the Intelligent Systems and Wireless Networking (IS-WiN) Laboratory, led by Dr. Fatemeh Afghah, focusing on advanced machine learning, artificial intelligence, and wireless communications. I am also completing my MSE in Electrical Engineering at The University of Alabama in Huntsville, expected in December 2025, also affiliated with the NIS Laboratory, advised by Dr. Dinh C. Nguyen.

I completed B.Sc. in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering from Daffodil International University in Dhaka under advisor Dr. Md. Taslim Arefin, and since then, I've been deeply involved in the complexities of ML, AI, privacy, and security, and Wireless Communication.

Since 2015, I have been an active IEEE member, contributing to the Bangladesh Chapter and the IEEE Region 3, Huntsville Section. My role in IEEE has allowed me to bridge communities and enhance student engagement through numerous conferences and events across Bangladesh, India, and Nepal.

I am passionate about creating platforms for intellectual exchange, demonstrated by my role as Head Organizer of TEDxUA Huntsville 2025, and my leadership as President of the UAH Bangladeshi Student Association (2024-2026) and Secretary of the UAH Graduate Student Association (2024-2025). I also serve as a peer reviewer for top-tier journals including IEEE Internet of Things Journal and ACM Computing Surveys.
