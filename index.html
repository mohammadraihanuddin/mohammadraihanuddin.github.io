Mohammad Raihan Uddin

PhD Student in Computer Engineering at Clemson University

Researcher in ML, AI, Privacy, and Security, Wireless Communications

I am a PhD student in Computer Engineering at Clemson University and an enthusiastic researcher at The Intelligent Systems and Wireless Networking (IS-WiN) Laboratory in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Clemson University under the supervision of Dr. Fatemeh Afghah.

I completed a Master's student in Electrical Engineering at The University of Alabama in Huntsville and an enthusiastic researcher at the Networking, Intelligence, and Security Lab under the supervision of Dr. Dinh C. Nguyen. And, a B.Sc in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering from Daffodil International University in Dhaka under advisor Dr. Md. Taslim Arefin, and since then, I've been deeply involved in the complexities of ML, AI, privacy, and security, and Wireless Communication.

Since 2015, I have been an active IEEE member, contributing to the Bangladesh Chapter and the IEEE Region 3, Huntsville Section. My role in IEEE has allowed me to bridge communities and enhance student engagement through numerous conferences and events across Bangladesh, India, and Nepal.

I am passionate about creating platforms for intellectual exchange, demonstrated by my role in organizing TEDxUA Huntsville 2025, and my leadership as the President of the UAH Bangladeshi Student Association and Secretary of the UAH Graduate Student Association.
