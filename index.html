Mohammad Raihan Uddin

Ph.D. Student in Computer Engineering, Clemson University

I am a Ph.D. student at Clemson University and a member of the IS-WiN Laboratory, advised by Dr. Fatemeh Afghah. I build trustworthy AI systems for wireless and distributed networks, with interests in federated learning, privacy, and AI-native 6G systems.

Email/ CV/ Google Scholar/ GitHub/ LinkedIn

Current focus: federated and quantum federated learning, secure AI for wireless systems, and LLM-assisted ORAN intelligence.

About

Before Clemson, I completed my MSE in Electrical Engineering at The University of Alabama in Huntsville, where I worked with Dr. Dinh C. Nguyen in the Networking, Intelligence, and Security (NIS) Lab. My research and projects span machine learning, privacy-preserving AI, and wireless communication systems.

I am also active in academic and professional communities through IEEE activities, technical event leadership, and peer reviewing.

Research Interests

  • Federated learning and quantum federated learning
  • Privacy-preserving and secure AI systems
  • AI for wireless communications and AI-native 6G
  • LLM-enhanced reasoning for Open RAN (ORAN)
